(posedge clock => (out +: in)) = 10;
        (negedge clock[0] => (out -: in)) = 10;
        (clock => (out : in)) = 10;
In these examples, + and - are polarity operators, and they indicate that data flows from in to out and is either inverted (-) or not inverted (+). In the absence of the polarity operator, there is no information about how out logically depends on in.
