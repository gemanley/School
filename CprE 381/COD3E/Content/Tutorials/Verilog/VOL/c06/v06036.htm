The force quasi-continuous assign statement has the same syntax as assign, but uses the force keyword: 

 
        force lhs = rhs;

The primary difference between force and the assign QCA is that the lhs of a force can be either a register or a net. If it is a register, then force acts very much like assign, the only difference being that force has higher precedence than assign.

If the lhs of the force is a net, then when the force is removed, the net reverts back to its "normal" value immediately, unlike a register, which will not change until the next procedural assignment.

The lhs may be a bit-select or part-select of a net, but not of a register.

The release statement is used to remove a force condition from a register or net:

        release lhs;
