All initial blocks begin at time 0 and execute the initial statement. Because the statement may be a compound statement, this may entail executing lots of statements. There may be time or event controls, as well as all of the control constructs in the language. As a result, an initial block may cause activity to occur throughout the entire simulation of the model.

When the initial statement finishes execution, the initial block terminates. If the initial statement is a compound statement, then the statement finishes after its last statement finishes.
