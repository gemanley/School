The built-in primitives are very straight-forward. The output port is listed first, and the input ports are listed last. The and, or, and xor primitives each have one output and may have as many input ports as desired. The buf and not primitives have as many output ports as desired, and only one input. Another difference between built-in primitives and other instances is that a built-in primitive may be instantiated without an instance name.

It is quite possible to have a module defined completely in terms of built-in primitives.
