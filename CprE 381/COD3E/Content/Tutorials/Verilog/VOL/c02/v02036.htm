Real numbers are represented in IEEE standard 754-1985 double precision format. Constants can be written in decimal notation or scientific notation. If a decimal point is present in the number, there must be at least one digit on either side of it. When real numbers are converted to integers, they are rounded, not truncated. Numbers which end in .5 are rounded away from zero. For example, 1.5 rounds to 2 and -1.5 rounds to -2.
