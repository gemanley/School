Exercises on a query language for objects

In Project 1 we considered a University application in the relational database setting. The following is a scheme for the same application in our own object-oriented setting. Luckily for us, every relation becomes a collection of objects. A Student is a person and a Faculty is also a person. These are shown as directed edges labeled "isa". All other directed edges show reference attributes. Note that PreReq attribute in Course is a set of references.

In our own SQL-like language, express each of the following in terms of single select statement. Of course, a select statement may have other nested selects. Note that our problem numbers deliberately start at 07. Problems 07-12 will directly correspond to Items 07-12 on a future project where you will be asked to write object-oriented style XQuery queries instead of our SQL-style queries.  FYI, Items 01 to 06 in that project will be about relational style XQuery queries.

Feel free to come-up with any constructs for your SQL queries so long as they make sense from a logical standpoint.

Problem 07. List the FacultyID, Rank and Salary of the faculty who is a professor with a salary of 100K or higher.

Problem 08. List the StudentIDs of students and the FacultyIDs of their Mentors for students who have got at least one A's in their grades.

Problem 09. List the names of students and the FacultyIDs of their Mentors for students who have got all A's in their grades.

Problem 10. List the course codes of those courses that are prerequisites of prerequisites for the course ‘CS511’. We only want second level prerequisites. You should ignore immediate prerequisites.

Problem 11. For each classification, list the number of students and average GPA. Your query should not use constants, such as "Freshman". In the output, each tuple should consist of Classification, NumOfStudents, and AvgGPA.

Problem 12. Reorganize Enrollment collection by StudentID. For each student you should have retrieve the student's id and a Grade. The Grade should be a relation consisting of CourseCode and LetterGrade attributes.
