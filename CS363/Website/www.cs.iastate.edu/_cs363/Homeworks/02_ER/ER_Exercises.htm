Exercises on Entity-relationship model

For each of Problems 1-4 below, refer to our Entity-Relationship scheme for the Personnel Database consisting of 4 entity sets, two relationships, and two occurrences of isa.

1. In an alternative design for the same application, show how the Manager entity can be eliminated and replaced by an attribute. Where will you place the attribute.

2. What changes in the application will make the alternative design in Exercise 1 unattractive?

3. Suppose that that a department can have 0 or more managers. A manager may manage 1 or more departments. In addition, each manager has a combined budget for all departments it serves. Show the necessary changes to our design.

4. Departments are allowed to participate in community causes. Every community cause has a CName. Add such information to our design.

5. Design an appropriate entity relationship scheme for the University database of Project 1. Briefly, the university application consists of persons, students, instructors, and courses. Some courses are offered and students enroll in such courses. More details about the application was implicit in the project statement.

6. (This continues Problem 5) Once you have designed the ER scheme, translate your scheme to a relational database scheme. It is enough to leave your design in terms of relation schemes and residual notes. It is not necessary to write the create table statements. In other words you complete first two phases in translation, but not the third one. Also, note that your ER scheme followed by your translation may or may not lead to the same relational scheme as in Project 1.
