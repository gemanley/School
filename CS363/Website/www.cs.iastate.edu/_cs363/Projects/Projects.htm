2. Procedure for submission of projects

A project is submitted in two stages: an Electronic Submission, followed by Hardcopy Submission. In statement of every project it will be clearly stated which files are to be submitted electronically and which files are to be submitted as hardcopy. For our policy on late submission, please see the course syllabus.

A. Procedure for electronic submission

  • For electronic submission of your work, you will need to have an Unix account with the CS Department. If you do not have an account, please submit a request at http://support.cs.iastate.edu/faq/account.
  • You also need a product called Secure Shell, a tool that you can use to connect to your Unix account in order to transfer files between your machine and your Unix account and execute Unix commands. If you do not have Secure Shell, please Click Here.

Step 1. Start Secure Shell client and connect to pyrite.cs.iastate.edu.

Step 2. Statement of every project will clearly list the files to be submitted electronically. Transfer these files to a folder on your Unix account. This folder should not contain any files that are not to be submitted electronically.

Step 3. Enter and execute one of the following turnin commands.  

turnin cs363 Fall2012/Project0 MyFolderForProject0 
turnin cs363 Fall2012/Project1 MyFolderForProject1 
turnin cs363 Fall2012/Project2 MyFolderForProject2 
turnin cs363 Fall2012/Project3 MyFolderForProject3 
turnin cs363 Fall2012/Project4 MyFolderForProject4 
(Note that the forward slashes "/" should be used in Unix)

Here, MyFolderForProject0 is the name of your folder containing the files to be submitted electronically. As stated above, please do not put any other material in that folder.

Example. Suppose you are submitting for Project 3 and your files are in the folder My363Work/P3 in your Unix user space, then the command to be executed is as follow:

turnin cs363 Fall2012/Project3 My363Work/P3

On execution, you will see the message "Turnin SUCCEEDED" to indicate success. When you repeat the process for submission for the same project, your old files are replaced with the new ones.

If you experience problems, please contact the TA listed first on the [Getting Help] page.  

B. Procedure for hardcopy submission

Statement of every project will clearly list the files to be submitted as hardcopy. Bring hardcopy of your files to the first lecture after your electronic submission. A coversheet must be used. If there are multiple documents, they should be collated and staples together in the listed order with the coversheet on the top. To obtain a coversheet, please click here.
