2. Syllabus

Course website

  • www.cs.iastate.edu/~cs363

Prerequisites

  • You must drop 363 immediately if you have not completed Com S 228 with a grade of C- or above and Engl 104.

Text book

  • A slide presentation in form of a book authored by Shashi K Gadia; available at Copyworks at $15.42 + tax. There will be one additional chapter on data warehousing toward the end of this term.

Projects (1 warm-up project and 4 real projects, count 30% )

  • Project 0, a warm-up project counting 2%, helps you set up the system and understand procedures
  • Projects 1 to 4 count 7% each
  • Collaboration or cheating on projects is forbidden

Homeworks (5; count 10%)

  • Homeworks supplement projects; together they prepare you for tests
  • Studying together on homeworks is encouraged.
  • Advise: play an active role in your study group.
  • Solutions must be written independently.
  • Homeworks prepare you for tests.

Tests (count 60%)

  • There will be two closed book tests and a closed book final. The final will be comprehensive. Your 2 best scores out of the three will be considered.

Attendance (a tricky business: please read carefully)

  • Attendance is required.
  • Up to 5 absences will be waived. Beyond that, up to 0.50 % may be deducted for each absence.
  • Will take genuine individual difficulties into account
  • (must see the instructor proactively, unless impossible)
  • The instructor will reserves complete discretion.
  • If you have problem with my policy ... please seek clarification now.
  • Do not complain if at the end of the semester ...
  • the policy is used very severely, or not used at all

Submission and late submission of work

  • Projects submission = electronic + hardcopy
  • Add coversheet to all homeworks & project hardcopies. A link to coversheet appears at the top of this page. 
  • Submit in beginning of class (preferred)
  • or drop-box outside 106 Atanasoff Hall
  • Daily cutoff time: 12:00 PM Noon Monday to Friday, and Sunday
  • Total of 6 virtual coupons to manage late submissions. One coupon allows one item to be submitted one day late. A weekend, including a long weekend, counts as one day. Entry for number of coupons should be made on Title Sheet. There will be some absolute deadlines (when you can not use coupons). Additional coupons can be negotiated in cases of genuine hardship (see instructor proactively unless impossible); the instructor reserves discretion
  • A deposit box for submissions of hardcopies of projects and homeworks has been placed next to 106 Atanasoff Hall. The box can be used when a due date is not coinciding with a lecture or for late submissions (when late submissions are allowed).

Communication and help

  • Instructor and a TA are almost always available at the end of lectures
  • Use the prefix "Com S 363..." in the subject of all your email
  • Email should not be used for debugging your code
  • Email does not substitute a visit
  • For policy, see the instructor; for projects, try to see TAs first
  • For correction in our records, see the TA who visits lectures

Pickup your graded work promptly

Comprehensive reports are provided after Test 1 and Test 2
